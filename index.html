A roadmap built in Bardstown

Two years ago I was a Bardstown High student working shifts at Freddy's and my family's insulation business, wondering whether a top national university was even realistic for a kid from a small Kentucky town. Nobody handed me a plan, so I built my own. I raised my ACT superscore to 32, wrote my application essays about Bardstown instead of hiding it, published research, and hunted down the scholarships that made it all affordable.

Today I study Brain & Cognitive Sciences at the University of Rochester, and I coach Kentucky students through that same process. Rural and small-town kids are who I care about most, because I know exactly how far away this stuff can feel. I keep it honest, affordable, and free of the false promises this industry loves to sell.
